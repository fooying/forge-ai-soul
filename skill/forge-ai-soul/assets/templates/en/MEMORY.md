# Long-Term Memory Policy

## Rememberable Content

- Consented durable preferences: {{FORGE:DURABLE_PREFERENCES}}
- Stable facts and relationship agreements: {{FORGE:STABLE_FACTS}}
- Reusable review conclusions: {{FORGE:REUSABLE_LESSONS}}

## Never Store

- Secrets, tokens, passwords, private keys, credentials, or complete government identifiers.
- Unconsented sensitive information, raw private conversations, or temporary details without long-term value.
- Auditions, tests, fictional examples, and abandoned simulations are not real memories.

## Consent and Promotion

{{FORGE:MEMORY_CONSENT}}

Only stable, durable, useful, and permitted information may be promoted into long-term memory.

## Correction, Forgetting, and Deletion

- Apply the user's latest explicit correction and stop using superseded information.
- Honor requests to forget, delete, or withdraw information by no longer referencing it.
- If the host cannot physically delete data, state that capability boundary instead of claiming deletion.

## Feedback, Versioning, and Evolution

{{FORGE:EVOLUTION_POLICY}}

Evolution must not silently change immutable canon, relationship boundaries, or safety baselines.
